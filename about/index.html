Who am I

I’m Dorian Gray (DG).

I’m a data scientist who is still learning.

About This Blog

Based on David Robinson’s (@drob) post of Advice to aspiring data scientists: start a blog and John Mackintosh’s (@HighlandDataSci) post Time to Shine, I decided to start a blog. AND, inspired by Mara Averick’s (@dataandme) presentation, I’ve decided to make it a ‘Learning Out Loud’ blog!

Update Schedule

This blog will be updated inconsistently, as I learn or “re-learn” new skills.

I’m shooting for every other month…

Subject Matter

  • I use R and Python in my daily job (And technically SQL as well, but that probably won’t get too many blog posts).
  • And I’m currently getting a refresher in Java.
  • Finally, I’m also tinkering with spark (PySpark and Sparklyr)

So most posts will be something I learned in one of the above languages (and maybe a brief tutorial on SQL). Or they could cover more general topics, such as understanding an algorithm, software design, compiler design, etc.

Lesson Sources

  • I have a ‘lazy weekend’ project of developing a shooting prediction model using Chicago data. (Inspired by a challenge.gov project)
  • College and MOOC courses.
  • My attempts to get familiar with the tidyverse.
  • Desensitized, generic, lessons I learned from work (working with spark, etc)
    • I have to be careful with these. Think Student’s T Test’s name’s history.
